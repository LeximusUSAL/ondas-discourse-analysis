What the Data Reveals

ONDAS dominates in technology

Listening devices (+497%) and act of listening (+47%). The radio lexicon (emisión, transmisión, retransmisión, radio, emisora, antena, galena) is almost exclusive to ONDAS. This confirms the nature of the magazine as a transmedia device: its discourse not only talks about music, but constructs its own vocabulary for the act of listening through radio technology.

El Sol dominates in live criticism

Audience response (+110%) and emotions and sensations (+37%). A newspaper covering live concerts speaks much more about applause, ovations, successes, triumphs and emotions than a radio programming magazine. El Sol reflects the concert experience as a social event: the physical presence of the audience, their collective reaction and the emotional judgement of the critic before the performance.

Auditory perception, aesthetic evaluation, and sound qualities are shared

The basic musical lexicon (belleza, melodía, armonía, ritmo, escuchar, oír) is common to both media with virtually identical densities. This suggests a shared discursive substrate: regardless of the media format, writing about music in early twentieth-century Spain shares a stable aesthetic and perceptive vocabulary, inherited from the tradition of nineteenth-century music criticism.
