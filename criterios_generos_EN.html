Musical Genres: Classification Criteria

ONDAS Corpus · Madrid · 1925-1935

LexiMus Project · MUSLYME · University of Salamanca · 2026

Method Opera Zarzuela Symphonic Chamber Song Flamenco Jazz Choral Religious Ballet Popular Operetta Excluded Overlaps

1. Method

The analysis of musical genres is based on the detection of key terms associated with each genre within the 472 TXT files of the corpus (1,743,435 words). For each genre, a set of representative words and expressions was defined. They were searched as whole words (delimited by \b in regular expression) in the full text of the corpus converted to lowercase.

Counts were carried out in two ways:

  • Global: total sum of occurrences across the entire corpus.
  • By year: sum of occurrences per file, grouped by year of publication.
Note: No semantic disambiguation was applied: each term was counted in all contexts in which it appears. Possible ambiguities are noted for each genre.

2.4. Chamber Music (total: 4,360 mentions)

Criterion: terms designating the main chamber ensembles and the generic name of the genre.

TermOccurrences
cuarteto684
trío459
quinteto612
sexteto2,311
música de cámara294
WARNING — «sexteto» (2,311 mentions): This figure is anomalously high. In the context of Unión Radio, the «Sexteto de Unión Radio» or «Sexteto de la Estación» was the resident musical ensemble that performed the daily programming. Most of the 2,311 occurrences probably refer to this permanent station ensemble, not to chamber music in the strict sense. Without «sexteto» mentions, the total would be 2,049 mentions.

Not included: «dúo» (ambiguous: may refer to two singers or a vocal piece), «sonata» (belongs to both chamber and solo repertoire), «septeto», «octeto», «noneto» (insignificant presence).

Ambiguity: «trío» may refer to the central section of a minuet or scherzo. «Cuarteto» may designate a vocal quartet as well as an instrumental one.

Ambiguity: «canción» is very generic. It is used for salon songs, folk songs, Lieder translated as «canciones», and light song. The count reflects the overall presence of the concept «song» in the magazine's discourse.

2.10. Ballet / Dance (total: 4,315 mentions)

Criterion: terms designating the main forms of stage dance and their performers.

TermOccurrences

WARNING — «danza» (1,662) and «baile» (1,392): These figures are high because both terms have very broad usage:
  • «Danza»: ballet or stage dance; dances within operas and zarzuelas; movements of instrumental suites; folk dances.
  • «Baile»: society dances; dance music (frequent blocks in radio programming); stage performances with dance.
The global figure does not exclusively reflect artistic stage dance, but the broad universe of bodily movement associated with music of the era.

Not included: «coreografía» (marginal), «pas de deux» (very specific), «tutú» (anachronistic).

2.11. Popular Music (total: 2,051 mentions)

Criterion: terms designating music of traditional roots or the aesthetic category of «popular» versus «art» music.

TermOccurrences
popular1,474

WARNING — «popular» (1,474) and «populares» (505): These adjectives have very broad usage:
  • Popular/traditional music as a genre.
  • «Canciones populares», «melodías populares» (folkloric sense).
  • «Popular» as a synonym for «well-known/famous» («the popular maestro», «the popular zarzuela»).
  • «Teatro popular», «fiesta popular», etc.
The actual number of mentions of popular music as a genre is probably considerably lower than 2,051. Reliable disambiguation would require manual context analysis or NLP.

Not included: «tradicional» (may refer to any tradition), «cancionero» (may be a book), «jota» (may be the letter of the alphabet), «sardana», «muñeira» (presence not verified).

The following relevant musical terms were not included in any genre category because they are cross-cutting or ambiguous:

marcha / marchasMay be military, funeral, wedding, etc.

4. Overlaps Between Categories

The categories are not mutually exclusive. A single text may contain terms from several genres simultaneously. Examples:

  • An article about an opera broadcast may contain «ópera» + «coro» + «ballet» + «danza» (ballet scenes within operas).
  • Radio programming schedules mix genres in a single block: «Sexteto de la Estación: [zarzuela] + [sinfonía] + [canción]».
  • «Popular» can qualify any other genre: «ópera popular», «zarzuela popular».
Note: The analysis quantifies lexical mentions, it does not classify articles by genre. An article with 5 mentions of «ópera» and 2 of «baile» contributes to both categories.
